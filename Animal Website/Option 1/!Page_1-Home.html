Nearly Threatened:

The classification of "Near Threatened" refers to species that could be threatened with extinction in the near future, but don't currently qualify for it. According to Wikipedia, "The rationale used for near-threatened [species] usually includes the criteria of vulnerabilities, which are plausible or nearly met, such as reduction in numbers or range."[1]

In laymen's terms, they're species that are on the brink of being threatened, but are barely scraping by.

Endangered:

The classification "Endangered" refers to species that are very likely to become extinct in the near future. According to Wikipedia, possible causes of getting to this classification include "habitat loss, poaching and invasive species"[2].

Many nations have different laws that protect these species. These laws do things such as forbid hunting, making protected areas, or restricting land development. Some endangered species are actively being helped with methods like captive breeding and habitat restoration.

*Claims here are based off of the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species

Conservation Status

All groups of organisms (like species) are categorized by something called Conservation Status. A species conservation status indicated whether it still exists and/or how likely it is to become extinct in the near future. Many factors are taken into account when assessing conservation status: not simply the number of individuals remaining, but the overall increase or decrease in the population over time, breeding success rates, and known threats. Various systems of conservation status exist and are in use at international, multi-country, national and local levels as well as for consumer use[3].
