My Experiences With Remote Learning

Overall Experiences.

Remote learning... hasn't been fun for me. I have moderate ADHD, which means focusing is extremely difficult. I'm behind in most of my classes, and haven't been able to get much work done.

Average Day

My average day honestly isn't anything special. I have 3 alarms set from 7am to 7:30am which I ignore. I get out of bed around 8am, and make a quick breakfast. After that, there's just school. I try my best to get assignments done and not get distracted. After school's over, I spent the next 3 hours attempting to do my homework. (see: staring at paper for 3 hours and/or getting distracted.)

The Pros and Cons of Remote Learning

Remote Learning has many pros and many cons.

✅Pros:

  • Time
  • Because of remote learning, the shcool has switched to block schedualing. This means we generally have more time to complete assignments.
  • A and B days
  • We can wake up later

❌Cons:

  • Time
  • Due to the increase in time we have to complete assignments, incentive to do those assignments on time decreases. Makes it harder to do work with adhd.
  • Bad enviorment
  • Can't focus
